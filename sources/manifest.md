## Live-search observations used but not fully captured

- During web research on `2026-04-15`, search-result snippets exposed a live `TwStalker` mirror result for `@TMBSPACESHIPS` showing the account with roughly `14K followers`, roughly `2K tweets`, and recent activity markers such as `a month ago` and `2 months ago`.
- That live-search observation cuts against the claim that the account stopped posting on `2026-02-27`, but it was not preserved as a local HTML capture because direct mirror access remained blocked.
- A different live-search snippet exposed profile text with AFIT / `AETC` / AFMC wording, while the local `TwiCopy` capture shows AFIT / `ATEC` / AFMC. That mirror disagreement is itself a reason for caution.
- The local `/tmbspaceships` repo uses app copy that says `583 tweets`, but its own code shows the real data file (`tmbspaceships_posts.json`) is gitignored and absent, and its X API client can fetch up to `3,200` tweets while excluding replies and retweets by default. That means `583` should be read as one local corpus size, not as a definitive statement about the account's total posting history.

## Key extracted facts

- The official Air Force biography says McCasland was commissioned in `1979`, held AFIT assignments at MIT, and was current as AFRL commander in `April 2013`.
- Riverside and Kirtland Partnership Committee pages say McCasland retired after a `34-year career` and later served as `Director of Technology` at ATA; KPC also identifies him as founder, owner, and president of `DBE Consulting LLC`.
- KOAT says he was last seen around `11:00 a.m.` on `2026-02-27`, and that there was `no evidence of foul play` as of `2026-03-06`.
- ABC and Fox transcripts add that a repairman interacted with him around `10:00 a.m.`, his wife returned around noon and found him gone, his phone and glasses were left behind, and missing items included hiking boots, a wallet, and a `.38-caliber revolver`.
- Fox's March 16 transcript adds that investigators publicly referenced `mental fog`, a search of a `Pagosa Springs` home, and a volume of `rabbit-hole` conspiracy tips.
- WikiLeaks email `3099` shows Tom DeLonge saying McCasland `helped assemble my advisory team`, while WikiLeaks emails `5078` and `9501` show direct replies from `Neil McCasland <neilmcc79@gmail.com>` in the same meeting thread.
- WikiLeaks email `2635` shows `Susan McCasland Wilkerson` accepted that same Podesta / DeLonge meeting invitation.
- iHeart metadata shows Ashton Forbes episodes that explicitly reference both McCasland and `TMBSpaceships`.
- The local `/tmbspaceships` repo shows a derived knowledge-graph app was built on a `583`-tweet corpus, but the underlying export file is not present; the fetch code can paginate up to `3,200` tweets and excludes replies / retweets by default, so repo corpus size should not be treated as total account output.
